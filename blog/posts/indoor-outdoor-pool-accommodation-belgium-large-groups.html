Practical Considerations

When evaluating accommodation with pools for large groups, consider practical factors beyond just whether pools exist.

Size: Pools need to accommodate your group size. For 30-40 people, small pools create congestion. Larger pools allow multiple people to use them simultaneously without feeling crowded.

Exclusive use: Pools are most valuable when your group has exclusive access. Shared pools with other guests create scheduling conflicts and reduce the natural gathering benefits that pools provide.

Year-round access: Indoor pools enable year-round usage. Outdoor-only pools limit access to warm weather, which means groups staying in cooler seasons might not have pool access.

Poolside areas: Pools need surrounding areas where people can relax, supervise, or socialize. Poolside space matters as much as pool size—people need areas to gather around pools, not just in them.
